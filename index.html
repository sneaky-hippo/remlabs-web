Why REM?

The science behind the metaphor.

During actual REM sleep, your brain doesn't rest — it consolidates memories, finds connections between things you experienced at different times, and discards noise. Neuroscientists call it offline processing. It's why a problem that had you stuck at midnight feels solvable by morning.

🧠
Memory consolidation

Your brain replays the day's inputs and decides what to keep. REM Labs does the same — ingesting everything from your apps and finding what's worth surfacing. In practice: things you saved weeks ago show up when they're actually relevant — not when you go looking.

🔗
Cross-source connection

REM sleep links memories from different times and contexts. The Dream Engine does this across your apps — connecting a Notion note from March to an email from yesterday. In practice: REM skips the noise and shows you the two things that actually moved your goals forward.

🌙
Signal over noise

REM sleep prunes irrelevant memories. The Dream Engine filters everything through your goals — so what surfaces is signal, not volume. In practice: you read one brief instead of checking five apps.
